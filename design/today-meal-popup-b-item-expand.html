Kal — design exploration, round 2

Popup B + tap a food for one serving

Variant B (centered card) with the two fixes: the ✕ now sits in the header row so it never covers the kcal, and the bottom Close button is gone (close = ✕, scrim, or Esc). New in this round: tap any food line to unfold what one serving is — its unit (g / oz where weighed) and its macros, straight from the food library. Three takes on that expansion below; everything is interactive and Breakfast is pre-opened with the eggs expanded so you can compare at a glance.

Variant B1
Quiet line
One muted mono line in a soft well: serving, kcal, colored P/C/F. The lightest touch — reads like a footnote, keeps the card feeling like an index card.

Variant B2
Chip strip
The serving and each macro become small pills, tinted with the app's P/C/F colors. Most scannable at arm's length; a touch more visual weight than B1.

Serving vs plate
