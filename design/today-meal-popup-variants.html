Meal detail popup, three ways

Tap any meal on the Today screen to see what it actually is — every item with its absolute amount (cooked + raw where relevant), pre-computed kcal, and the meal's macro totals. The checkbox still marks eaten with one tap; the popup is for reading the plan, and carries its own Mark-eaten button so nothing gets slower. All three variants use today's real plan data.

Variant A
Bottom sheet
iOS-native slide-up sheet with a grabber. Feels like a PWA should on a phone; thumb-reachable actions; dismiss by tapping the scrim. The list stays visible behind it, dimmed.
MealsTap for details
Variant B
Centered card
A dialog card floating over the dimmed list — the most “popup” of the three. Reads like an index card for the meal; close via ✕ or scrim. Slightly more formal, maximum focus on one meal.
MealsTap for details
Variant C
Inline expand
No overlay at all — the row unfolds in place, accordion-style, and the chevron rotates. Lightest touch: nothing covers the screen, several meals can be open at once, zero context switch.
